Our Procedures

Laser Vision Correction Options

We offer all major laser vision correction platforms — from the latest flapless SMILE Pro to proven Contoura LASIK.

Latest Generation
SMILE Pro

The most advanced laser vision correction available. SMILE Pro uses a femtosecond laser to create a tiny lens-shaped disc inside the cornea which is then removed through a 3mm keyhole incision — completely flapless. Zero flap complications, minimal dry eye, fastest recovery.

  • Flapless — no corneal flap created
  • 10-second laser treatment per eye
  • Minimal dry eye post-procedure
  • Safe for contact sports athletes
  • Back to work next day
Most Precise
Topo-Guided LASIK (Contoura)

Maps 22,000 data points on your corneal surface to treat imperfections beyond your glasses prescription — delivering superior optical quality. Many patients achieve better than 20/20 vision. Ideal for those with corneal irregularities.

  • 22,000-point corneal mapping
  • Treats HOAs beyond standard prescription
  • Superior night vision quality
  • Better results than standard LASIK
  • Day-care procedure
Proven Choice
Standard LASIK

Time-tested, highly effective laser vision correction. A thin flap is created in the corneal surface, laser reshaping is performed, and the flap is repositioned. Excellent results at a more accessible price point.

  • Proven 30+ year track record
  • Rapid recovery (24-48 hours)
  • Treats myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism
  • Most affordable option
Specialist Option
PRK / LASEK

Surface ablation technique — no flap, epithelium gently removed. Ideal for patients with thinner corneas, contact sport athletes, or patients who don't qualify for LASIK or SMILE. Recovery is slightly longer but results are excellent.

  • No corneal flap — safest for thin corneas
  • Ideal for rugby, boxing, martial arts
  • No flap trauma risk
  • Excellent long-term outcomes

Your Journey

The LASIK Process — Step by Step

1
Free Candidacy Screening

Comprehensive eye test including corneal topography, pachymetry, aberrometry, pupil measurement, and refraction. Takes about 60–90 minutes. We tell you honestly if you are suitable.

2
Corneal Mapping

If you're suitable, a detailed corneal scan maps your unique eye shape. This data is programmed into the laser system for your personalised treatment plan.

3
Laser Treatment (10–60 seconds)

You lie comfortably, anaesthetic drops are instilled. The entire laser procedure takes seconds per eye. You remain awake and aware but feel no pain — just gentle pressure.

4
Rest (30 minutes)

You rest in a recovery area. Eyes may feel gritty or watery. This is normal and settles within a few hours. A friend or family member should drive you home.

5
See Clearly — Next Morning

Most patients wake up the next morning seeing clearly without glasses. A follow-up check on Day 1, Week 1, Month 1, and 3 months ensures your recovery is on track.
