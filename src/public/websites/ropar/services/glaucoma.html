Understanding Glaucoma

The Silent Thief of Sight

1 in 8 people over 40 are at risk

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve — the connection between your eye and your brain. The most common type is caused by elevated pressure inside the eye (intraocular pressure, or IOP).

What makes glaucoma particularly dangerous is that it causes no pain and no early symptoms. By the time most patients notice vision loss, significant and irreversible nerve damage has already occurred. This is why it's called the "silent thief of sight."

In rural Punjab, glaucoma awareness remains low. Many patients are diagnosed only when significant damage has already occurred — making early screening essential.

Glaucoma Warning Signs

  • →
    Gradual loss of peripheral vision

    Loss of side vision — often noticed late

  • →
    Tunnel vision in advanced stages

    Central vision affected late — by which time severe damage exists

  • →
    Halos around lights at night

    Especially in acute angle-closure glaucoma

  • →
    Eye pain + redness + blurred vision

    Acute angle-closure — EMERGENCY. Seek care immediately

Most Common Type: No Symptoms at All

Primary open-angle glaucoma — the most prevalent type — is completely painless and symptom-free until advanced. Annual IOP checks after age 40 are essential.

What is MIGS?

A Revolution in Glaucoma Surgery

MIGS (Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery) represents the biggest advance in glaucoma treatment in decades — safer, faster, and less disruptive than traditional surgery.

Traditional Surgery vs MIGS

Traditional Trabeculectomy

  • ✗External drainage surgery
  • ✗4–6 weeks recovery
  • ✗Higher complication risk
  • ✗Frequent follow-up required

MIGS (iStent / Hydrus)

  • ✓Tiny device implanted inside the eye
  • ✓Same-day procedure — goes home same day
  • ✓Excellent safety profile
  • ✓Can be combined with cataract surgery

MIGS devices like iStent Inject W and Hydrus are tiny implants that work within the eye's natural drainage pathway (Schlemm's canal) to reduce intraocular pressure. They are typically implanted during cataract surgery — treating two conditions in a single operation.

The goal of all glaucoma treatment — whether drops, laser, or surgery — is to reduce IOP and prevent further optic nerve damage. MIGS achieves this with significantly less risk and faster recovery than traditional surgical methods.

Key benefit: MIGS does not burn bridges — if further surgery is needed in the future, it remains an option. Traditional trabeculectomy forecloses some future surgical options.

All Treatment Options

Other Glaucoma Treatments

Eye Drops

First-line treatment for most patients. Prostaglandin analogues, beta-blockers, and combination drops. We review adherence and side effects carefully.

SLT Laser

Selective Laser Trabeculoplasty — a painless outpatient laser that improves drainage. Often used as first-line treatment or when drops are not tolerated.

Trabeculectomy

Traditional filtration surgery for advanced glaucoma not controlled by other means. Reserved for cases where MIGS is not appropriate — performed with full aftercare support.

Are You at Risk?

Glaucoma Risk Factors

If you have one or more of these risk factors, you should have a glaucoma assessment every year.

👴
Age Over 40

Risk doubles every decade after 40

👨‍👩‍👦
Family History

First-degree relatives with glaucoma: 4-9x higher risk

🩸
Diabetes

Diabetics have significantly higher risk of open-angle glaucoma

👓
High Myopia

Short-sighted people (especially >-6D) are at elevated risk

🇮🇳
South Asian Ethnicity

South Asians have higher rates of angle-closure glaucoma

👁️
Previous Eye Injury

Trauma to the eye can cause secondary glaucoma years later
