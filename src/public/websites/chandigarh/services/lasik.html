Three Proven Roads to Glasses-Free Vision

Not every eye is the same. BMH offers three advanced laser vision correction procedures — Dr. Baweja will recommend the optimal choice based on your corneal anatomy, lifestyle, and prescription.

Most Popular

Blade-Free LASIK

The gold standard of vision correction. A femtosecond laser creates a precise corneal flap — no blade, no mechanical microkeratome — followed by an excimer laser that reshapes the cornea to your exact prescription. Clear vision typically achieved by the next morning.

25+ year proven track record worldwide
Next-day visual recovery for most patients
Suitable for most prescriptions (up to -10D)
Requires adequate corneal thickness ≥480µm
10–15 minutes per eye
Flapless

CLEAR

Corneal Lenticule Extraction Refractive — a completely flapless procedure ideal for patients with active lifestyles, contact sports, or those at risk of flap complications. A small lenticule of corneal tissue is extracted through a tiny incision, reshaping vision without creating a flap at all.

No flap — zero flap-related complications
Ideal for sports, military, physically active patients
Significantly lower dry eye incidence post-surgery
Excellent refractive stability long-term
Suitable for myopia and astigmatism
Premium

SMILE

Small Incision Lenticule Extraction — the most advanced keyhole laser vision correction technique. A 2–3mm arc incision allows extraction of a precisely cut corneal lenticule with minimal disruption to the corneal surface and nerves, preserving maximum biomechanical integrity.

Smallest incision of any laser procedure (2–3mm)
Minimal corneal nerve disruption
Premium choice for high myopia
Lowest post-op dry eye risk of all procedures
No flap, no blade, no excimer laser required
Step by Step

The LASIK Journey at BMH

From your first consultation to waking up with clear vision — every stage is precisely mapped, guided, and monitored by our surgical team.

1

Comprehensive Eye Mapping

Corneal topography, Pentacam analysis, pachymetry (corneal thickness), pupil diameter, dry eye assessment, and full refraction. This 60-minute workup determines candidacy and builds your personalised laser ablation profile.

60 min · Consultation day
2

Femtosecond Flap Creation

A computer-guided femtosecond laser creates a precisely thin, uniform corneal flap in under 20 seconds. No blade, no cutting wheel. The flap thickness and diameter are customised to your anatomy.

~20 seconds · Surgery day
3

Custom Excimer Laser Reshaping

The excimer laser precisely reshapes your corneal stroma using your personalised ablation profile — correcting myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism simultaneously with sub-micron accuracy and live eye-tracking.

45–90 seconds · Surgery day
4

Flap Repositioned — Vision Begins

The flap is gently repositioned. It self-seals within seconds through natural adhesion. No stitches. You rest for 30 minutes, then go home. Most patients notice dramatically improved vision within hours.

Day 1 review next morning

Total procedure time per eye: 10–15 minutes

Am I a LASIK Candidate?

A thorough pre-surgical assessment is mandatory — not every eye is suitable for every procedure. Here's a general guide:

Generally Suitable for LASIK

Green Light Criteria
Age 18 years or older (ideally 21+ for stability)
Stable prescription for at least 12 consecutive months
Adequate corneal thickness (typically ≥480µm)
Normal corneal topography (no irregularities)
Myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism within treatable range
No significant dry eye disease on assessment
Not pregnant or breastfeeding at time of procedure

Needs Individual Assessment

Needs Review
Thin corneas — may be redirected to SMILE or CLEAR instead
Keratoconus or irregular corneal astigmatism
Moderate to severe dry eye syndrome (treat first, then reassess)
Active corneal disease (herpes keratitis, corneal ectasia)
Certain autoimmune conditions (lupus, rheumatoid arthritis — assessed individually)
Pupil size concerns in low-light conditions
Very high prescriptions may require alternative correction (ICL)

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your LASIK consultation at BMH.

How is blade-free LASIK different from traditional LASIK?

Traditional LASIK used a mechanical microkeratome — a small oscillating blade — to create the corneal flap. Blade-free LASIK replaces this entirely with a femtosecond laser, which creates the flap with far greater precision, consistent thickness, and individually tailored geometry. The femtosecond flap has a more uniform architecture, better seals, and a significantly lower rate of flap complications including buttonholing, free caps, and irregular flaps. At BMH we exclusively use femtosecond technology — blade-based LASIK is not performed.

Is LASIK permanent?

Yes — LASIK permanently reshapes your cornea. The correction itself does not "wear off." However, presbyopia (age-related near vision loss) will develop naturally in your 40s regardless of LASIK, and some patients develop mild regression over years, particularly those with very high prescriptions. For most patients with stable prescriptions, 20+ year studies show durable excellent vision. Any regression can be assessed for an enhancement procedure if required.

Does LASIK hurt?

LASIK is performed under topical anaesthetic (numbing drops). The procedure itself is painless — you will feel pressure from the suction ring during flap creation, and some patients notice a mild smell during the laser reshaping step. Post-operatively, it is common to experience a gritty or burning sensation for 4–6 hours, which resolves with rest. Pain after LASIK is uncommon — most patients find the experience much more comfortable than they anticipated.

Can I have both eyes done on the same day?

Yes, bilateral simultaneous LASIK (both eyes on the same day) is the standard approach at BMH and worldwide. The risk of any serious intraoperative complication is so low that sequential same-day treatment is both safe and practical. It means one recovery period, one set of post-operative drops, and one day off work. Single-eye procedures are reserved for specific clinical reasons.

What is the difference between LASIK, CLEAR, and SMILE?

All three permanently correct your vision — the difference is the surgical approach. LASIK creates a flap that is lifted to allow excimer laser reshaping, then replaced. CLEAR is flapless — it extracts a lenticule through a tiny keyhole incision with no flap whatsoever. SMILE also extracts a lenticule but through an even smaller arc incision (2–3mm). CLEAR and SMILE are preferred for active patients, those in contact sports, patients with slightly thinner corneas, or those wanting the lowest dry eye risk. Dr. Baweja will recommend the right procedure for your anatomy at your assessment.

What is the minimum age for LASIK?

The minimum age is 18 years, but we typically prefer patients to be 21 or older and to have had a stable prescription for at least 12 months. This is because the eye can continue to change through the late teens and early 20s, and performing LASIK before prescription stability is established increases the risk of regression. There is no upper age limit for LASIK — the procedure is appropriate throughout adulthood, though older patients may also need to plan for presbyopia correction.
