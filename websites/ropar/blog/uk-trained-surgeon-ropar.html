Why a UK NHS Cambridgeshire-Trained Surgeon Chose to Come Home to Ropar

April 2026 · 5 min read · Dr. Varun Baweja

People sometimes ask me: "You trained at UK NHS Cambridgeshire — the best eye hospital in the world. Why did you come back to Ropar?" It's a question I'm proud to answer.

Growing Up Watching Preventable Blindness

I grew up in Punjab, and I grew up watching something that troubled me deeply: patients losing their vision to conditions that were completely treatable — if only they had access to the right care. Cataracts going untreated for years. Glaucoma undetected until it was too late. Patients travelling hundreds of kilometres to Chandigarh or Delhi for surgeries that should have been available closer to home.

This stayed with me. It was the reason I chose ophthalmology. It was the reason I decided that whatever I learned, I would bring it back.

UK NHS Cambridgeshire — What That Training Added

UK NHS Cambridgeshire experience added exposure to structured audit, protocol-led clinical decision making, complex ophthalmology cases, and patient-centred counselling.

My UK NHS experience in Cambridgeshire gave me exposure to the most complex cases, the most advanced surgical techniques, and the most rigorous clinical standards. I trained alongside global leaders in glaucoma, cataract surgery, cornea, and MIGS. I learned not just technique — but the mindset of NHS medicine: patient-centred, evidence-based, honest.

"Patients in Ropar deserve access to advanced, evidence-based eye care close to home. Geography should not determine the quality of your eyesight."

— Dr. Varun Baweja

Bringing History-Making Procedures to Punjab

When I returned to India, I was determined to do more than just practise — I wanted to advance the field in my home country. In 2021, I performed the first iStent Inject W procedure in India — a minimally invasive glaucoma surgery that had been available in the UK and US for years, but not yet introduced in India. The iStent Inject allows us to treat glaucoma at the same time as cataract surgery, through the same incision, with the same anaesthetic.

The following year, I introduced the Hydrus Microstent to our region — becoming the first surgeon in the area to offer this advanced MIGS procedure. The results have been outstanding for our patients — many of whom were previously managing multiple glaucoma drops with poor quality of life.

NHS Values in Punjab

The NHS taught me something beyond surgical skill: it taught me how to be a doctor. NHS medicine is fundamentally about the patient — not the procedure, not the revenue. You tell patients honestly what they need, you don't recommend surgery unless it's in their best interest, and you treat every patient with the same respect regardless of their background or ability to pay.

I brought these values back to Baweja Hospital Ropar. We will never recommend an operation unless it genuinely benefits you. We discuss every option — including doing nothing — with honesty. We price transparently, with no hidden costs. And we take the time to explain, in plain language, what is happening with your eyes.

The Work is Not Done

Ropar and the surrounding districts of Rupnagar, Morinda, Nangal, and Anandpur Sahib have enormous unmet need. Many patients still travel to Chandigarh or Delhi for eye surgery — unaware that they can receive the same quality, or better, right here at home. Many more have never had their eyes checked at all.

We run regular free eye camps, school vision screenings, and diabetic retinopathy drives across the region. We partner with local PHCs for referral and follow-up. We are committed to raising awareness of preventable blindness in rural Punjab — because awareness is the first step.

If you or a family member needs an eye assessment, cataract surgery, LASIK, or glaucoma treatment — come and see us at Baweja Hospital Ropar. NHS-informed care, right here at home. Call +91-81466-22802 or book online.

A Promise to Punjab

Every morning I walk into our operating theatre in Ropar, I think about why I came back. The patients who have regained their vision. The elderly farmer who sees his grandchildren clearly for the first time in years. The young professional who threw away her glasses after SMILE Pro. The glaucoma patient whose pressure is now perfectly controlled with an iStent implant, years after being told they would need multiple drops forever.

This is why I came home. And I wouldn't have it any other way.

Dr. Varun Baweja, MS Ophthalmology, MRCPS Glasgow, Advanced Fellowship UK NHS Cambridgeshire. Consultant Ophthalmologist & MIGS Pioneer, Baweja Hospital Ropar.
