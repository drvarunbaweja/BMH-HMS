Glaucoma: The Silent Thief of Sight — Why Every Punjabi Over 40 Should Get Screened

Dr. Varun Baweja — Moorfields NHS Trained · First in India: iStent Inject
Consultant Ophthalmologist & MIGS Glaucoma Surgeon, Baweja Hospital Ropar

There is a condition that is silently stealing the vision of thousands of people across Punjab right now — and most of them have no idea it's happening. I'm talking about glaucoma, and as a surgeon who performed India's first iStent Inject procedure, I want every family in Rupnagar, Morinda, Anandpur Sahib and the surrounding districts to understand this disease.

⚠️ Important: Glaucoma causes no pain and no early symptoms. By the time you notice vision loss, significant and irreversible nerve damage has already occurred. The only way to detect glaucoma early is a regular eye pressure (IOP) check.

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of conditions that damage the optic nerve — the cable connecting your eye to your brain. Most cases are caused by elevated pressure inside the eye (intraocular pressure, or IOP). When IOP is too high for too long, it slowly destroys the optic nerve fibres, causing irreversible vision loss — starting from the periphery and eventually leading to complete blindness if untreated.

The most common type — Primary Open Angle Glaucoma (POAG) — is completely painless and symptom-free in its early and moderate stages. This is why glaucoma is called the "silent thief of sight." Patients look and feel completely normal while their optic nerve is being gradually destroyed.

The Punjab Picture — Why This Matters Here

India has the world's second-largest number of glaucoma patients — an estimated 12 million people are affected. In Punjab, several factors increase risk:

  • High diabetes prevalence: Diabetics have significantly elevated glaucoma risk
  • Genetic factors: South Asian ethnicity carries higher rates of angle-closure glaucoma
  • Low awareness: Most rural patients are unaware of glaucoma or its silent nature
  • Limited screening: Regular IOP checks are not yet part of routine health check culture

Who is at Risk?

You are at elevated risk of glaucoma if you have any of the following:

  • Age over 40 (risk doubles every decade)
  • First-degree relative (parent, sibling, child) with glaucoma
  • Diabetes mellitus — any type
  • High myopia (short-sightedness, especially over -6D)
  • Previous significant eye injury
  • Long-term steroid use (eye drops, tablets, or inhaled)
  • South Asian or African-Caribbean ethnicity

If you have one or more of these risk factors, please get an annual IOP check. It takes 5 minutes and could save your sight.

The Revolution: MIGS — Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery

When I returned from my Fellowship at Moorfields Eye Hospital NHS London, I was determined to bring the most advanced glaucoma treatments to Punjab. The most exciting development in glaucoma surgery in decades is MIGS — Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery.

Traditional glaucoma surgery (trabeculectomy) creates an external drainage bleb and carries significant risks. MIGS devices like iStent Inject W and Hydrus Microstent work within the eye's natural drainage system, dramatically reducing IOP with minimal risk and same-day recovery.

iStent Inject W — First in India

I was proud to perform the first iStent Inject W procedure in India in 2021. This tiny titanium device — smaller than a grain of rice — is implanted during cataract surgery and creates a bypass through the trabecular meshwork, restoring the eye's natural drainage. Clinical studies show 20–30% IOP reduction that is sustained long-term.

Hydrus Microstent — First in Region

The Hydrus is an 8mm nitinol scaffold that dilates Schlemm's canal across 3 clock hours. The landmark HORIZON trial demonstrated that 77% of Hydrus patients were medication-free at 5 years. I introduced this procedure to our region — and results have been remarkable.

Key MIGS advantage: Both iStent and Hydrus are performed simultaneously with cataract surgery — treating two sight-threatening conditions (cataract + glaucoma) in a single operation, with a single recovery.

What Does a Glaucoma Assessment Involve?

A comprehensive glaucoma assessment at Baweja Hospital Ropar includes:

  1. Intraocular pressure measurement (Goldman tonometry)
  2. Optic nerve OCT imaging — structural analysis of nerve fibre layer
  3. Automated visual field testing — functional assessment
  4. Corneal pachymetry — thickness affects IOP measurement
  5. Gonioscopy — drainage angle assessment
  6. Optic disc photography — baseline documentation

The full assessment takes 60–90 minutes and is completely painless. If glaucoma is detected, we discuss your treatment options honestly and without pressure.

Treatment Is Not a Life Sentence

Many patients fear that a glaucoma diagnosis means a lifetime of drops and deteriorating vision. This is not true. With modern treatment — especially MIGS in suitable patients — most people with glaucoma live normal lives and retain good vision indefinitely, provided the condition is detected early and managed well.

The key word is early. Please don't wait for symptoms. There are none.
