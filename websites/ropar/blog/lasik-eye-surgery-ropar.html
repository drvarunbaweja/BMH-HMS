LASIK Eye Surgery in Ropar — Everything You Need to Know Before Taking the Plunge

Dr. Varun Baweja
MS Ophthalmology · MRCPS Glasgow · Moorfields Eye Hospital NHS, London

If you're tired of reaching for your glasses every morning, squinting at screens, or fumbling with contact lenses — you've probably wondered whether LASIK is right for you. As a surgeon who trained at Moorfields Eye Hospital NHS in London and now performs SMILE Pro and topo-guided LASIK right here in Ropar, I want to give you an honest, comprehensive guide to laser vision correction.

What Exactly is LASIK?

LASIK (Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis) is a refractive surgery that reshapes the cornea — the clear front surface of the eye — to correct how light focuses on the retina. By changing the corneal curvature, we can correct myopia (short-sightedness), hyperopia (long-sightedness), and astigmatism. The result, for most patients, is the ability to see clearly without glasses or contact lenses.

Modern LASIK has evolved enormously since its introduction in the 1990s. Today's options include SMILE Pro (completely flapless), Topo-Guided LASIK (which maps 22,000 points on your cornea), and conventional LASIK — each with specific advantages.

SMILE Pro: The Newest Generation

SMILE Pro (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) is the most advanced laser vision correction available. Unlike traditional LASIK, no corneal flap is created. Instead, a femtosecond laser creates a tiny lens-shaped disc (lenticule) inside the cornea, which is then removed through a 3mm keyhole incision.

Why patients prefer SMILE Pro:

  • Completely flapless — no flap complications possible
  • 10-second laser treatment per eye
  • Significantly less dry eye compared to LASIK
  • Safe for contact sports and physically active patients
  • Most patients see clearly within 24 hours

Dr. Varun's tip: If you play contact sports, box, or do martial arts — SMILE Pro is almost always my first recommendation. The absence of a corneal flap means there's no risk of flap dislodgement from a physical impact.

Topo-Guided LASIK (Contoura Vision)

Topo-guided LASIK uses corneal topography to map 22,000 points on the corneal surface. This allows the laser to treat not just your glasses prescription, but also subtle corneal irregularities called higher-order aberrations (HOAs). The result is often sharper, crisper vision than standard LASIK — many patients achieve better than 20/20.

This is my preference for patients with corneal irregularities, previous contact lens-induced changes, or those seeking the highest optical quality — particularly for night driving.

Am I a Candidate? The Honest Checklist

Not everyone is suitable for LASIK, and I believe in being completely honest about this. Here are the key criteria:

  • Age: 18 or older, with stable vision for at least 12 months
  • Prescription: Myopia up to -10D, hyperopia up to +4D, astigmatism up to 6D (depending on corneal thickness)
  • Corneal thickness: Sufficient tissue must remain after treatment (measured by pachymetry)
  • No active dry eye: Manageable dry eye is treatable; severe dry eye may be a contraindication
  • No keratoconus: Corneal topography screening rules this out
  • No pregnancy: We wait until 3 months after delivery or breastfeeding ends

The only way to know for certain is a comprehensive screening assessment, which I offer free of charge at Baweja Hospital Ropar. I will never recommend surgery if you are not a suitable candidate.

The Procedure: What to Expect

The entire process on your surgery day takes approximately 1.5–2 hours, though the laser treatment itself takes just seconds per eye:

  1. Preparation: Anaesthetic drops instilled, eye marked, patient positioned
  2. Laser treatment: 10 seconds (SMILE Pro) to 60 seconds (LASIK) per eye
  3. Recovery: 30 minutes rest, then discharge with a friend or family member driving

There is no pain during the procedure. You may feel gentle pressure for a few seconds. The experience is very different from what people imagine when they think of "eye surgery."

Recovery and What to Expect

Most SMILE Pro patients wake up the next morning seeing clearly — sometimes better than they've seen in years. LASIK recovery is similarly rapid. Common experiences in the first 24–48 hours:

  • Mild grittiness or watering (completely normal)
  • Slight light sensitivity (wear sunglasses outdoors)
  • Slight fluctuation in vision (settles over 1–2 weeks)

Most patients can drive and return to desk work within 24–48 hours. Full stabilisation of vision takes 4–8 weeks.

Is LASIK Permanent?

Yes — the corneal reshaping is permanent. However, as with all surgical procedures, there is a small chance (2–5%) of regression over time, particularly in higher prescriptions. Enhancement procedures are available if needed.

One important caveat: presbyopia (age-related near vision loss after 45) is a natural process that affects everyone — whether or not they have LASIK. After 45, most patients will need reading glasses regardless, even with excellent distance vision.
