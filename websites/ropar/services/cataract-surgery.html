What is Cataract?

Understanding Your Condition

A cataract is a clouding of the natural lens inside the eye. It is the leading cause of reversible blindness in India — and completely curable with a short surgical procedure.

Common Symptoms

  • →Blurred or hazy vision, like looking through frosted glass
  • →Difficulty driving at night, halos around lights
  • →Frequent need to change glasses prescription
  • →Fading or yellowing of colours
  • →Double vision in one eye

When cataract begins interfering with daily activities, it is time to consider surgery. There is no medication that reverses cataract — surgery is the only cure, and modern techniques make it one of the safest operations in medicine.

When to Operate?

Surgery is recommended when vision loss affects your quality of life — reading, driving, cooking, or recognising faces. Modern phacoemulsification does not require the cataract to be "ripe" — early treatment gives better outcomes.

🇬🇧 NHS Guideline

The NHS recommends cataract surgery when vision impairment significantly impacts daily life — you do not need to wait until vision is "very bad."

Surgical Options

Types of Cataract Surgery

Standard
MSICS

Manual Small Incision Cataract Surgery — proven, affordable, and highly effective. Ideal for advanced cataracts. Suitable for all patients. Delivered with precision and care.

Most Popular
Phacoemulsification

Micro-incision (2.4 mm) stitchless technique using ultrasonic energy to dissolve the cataract. Rapid recovery — most patients see clearly the next morning. This is the global gold standard.

Premium
Laser-Assisted (Femto)

Computer-guided laser precision for incision creation and nuclear fragmentation. Maximum accuracy, reduced phaco energy, ideal for premium IOL implantation.

Intraocular Lenses

Choose Your Perfect IOL

Your artificial lens is implanted permanently. We help you choose the option that best matches your lifestyle and visual goals.

👁️
Monofocal IOL

Excellent distance vision. Reading glasses needed for near tasks. Most affordable, time-tested option.

✨
Multifocal IOL

See clearly at distance, intermediate and near — significantly reduces spectacle dependence. Premium lifestyle lens.

🎯
Toric IOL

Corrects astigmatism simultaneously during cataract surgery. Sharper, cleaner distance vision for astigmatic patients.

🔭
EDOF IOL

Extended Depth of Focus — a continuous range of vision with minimal glare or halos. Ideal for active, working-age patients.

Your Journey

The Cataract Surgery Process

1
Consultation & Eye Assessment

Comprehensive eye examination including visual acuity, slit lamp assessment, IOL power calculation (biometry), corneal topography, and retinal evaluation.

2
Pre-Operative Tests

Blood sugar, blood pressure, ECG (if required), and final IOL selection. Our team explains every option clearly so you can choose with confidence.

3
Surgery (15 Minutes)

Performed under local anaesthetic drops — no injections required for most patients. Completely painless. You remain awake but relaxed throughout the short procedure.

4
Recovery Room (30 min)

Post-operative monitoring, eye shield placed, initial vision assessment. Our nursing team guides you on eye drop regime and care instructions.

5
Discharge Same Day

You go home the same day — day-care surgery. Most patients notice dramatic vision improvement by the next morning. Follow-up at Day 1, Week 1, Month 1.

Cataract Surgery FAQs

Modern cataract surgery is completely painless. Anaesthetic drops are instilled into the eye — no injections are required for most patients. You may feel mild pressure during the procedure but no pain. The operation typically takes just 10–15 minutes.
Most patients notice a dramatic improvement in vision within 24 hours of surgery. Vision continues to stabilise over the first 4–6 weeks as the eye heals. The speed of recovery depends on your IOL type and how your eye responds.
Dr. Varun will discuss your daily activities, occupation, driving needs, and reading habits before recommending an IOL. There is no universal "best" lens — the right choice depends on your lifestyle. He will give you an honest recommendation with no pressure toward higher-cost options.
