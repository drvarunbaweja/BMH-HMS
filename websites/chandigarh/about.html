OUR STORY

From Ropar in 1984
to Chandigarh in 2022

The Chandigarh centre is new, but the Baweja name carries decades of clinical trust. Our first hospital began in Ropar as a women and maternity centre, later growing into eye care, psychiatry, cosmetology and multispeciality services before the Chandigarh journey began in 2022.

Contact Us
THE BAWEJA JOURNEY

A New Chandigarh Centre, Backed by a Longer Family Legacy

Patients should immediately understand both facts: the Chandigarh branch opened in 2022, and the family hospital journey began much earlier in Ropar.

Ropar Hospital Started in 1984

The first Baweja hospital in Ropar began primarily as a gynaecology and maternity centre, serving families through pregnancy, delivery and women's health.

Expanded Multispeciality Care in 2012

Ophthalmology, psychiatry, cosmetology and other specialties were added, turning the Ropar centre into a broader family healthcare destination.

Chandigarh Centre Opened in 2022

After UK NHS Cambridgeshire experience, the Chandigarh centre brought advanced eye care, LASIK, MIGS glaucoma, gynaecology, psychiatry and aesthetics to Tricity patients.

MILESTONES

From One Centre to Two Patient Communities

Every milestone represents continuity: a Ropar foundation, expanded specialist experience, and the Chandigarh centre created for modern, appointment-led care.

1984
First Baweja Hospital in Ropar

The Ropar hospital began primarily as a gynaecology centre, building trust with families through maternity care, women's health and accessible local service.

2012
Specialities Added at Ropar

Eye care, psychiatry, cosmetology and other services were added, creating a multispeciality foundation before the Chandigarh branch was launched.

2022
Chandigarh Centre Opened

BMH Chandigarh opened after UK NHS Cambridgeshire experience, with advanced eye care, women's health, neuropsychiatry and aesthetics for Tricity patients.

2024
Advanced Cataract and Glaucoma Pathways

Chandigarh became a focused destination for premium cataract counselling, glaucoma monitoring, minimally invasive glaucoma surgery education, and laser vision correction assessment.

2020s
MIGS Glaucoma — First in Chandigarh · 7 Specialities · 500+ Weekly Patients

MIGS (Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery) was launched at BMH — the first hospital in Chandigarh to offer this procedure. The hospital expanded to seven clinical specialities, now serving over 500 patients weekly with a team of six specialist doctors.

Today
Baweja Multispeciality Hospital — Chandigarh's Healthcare Home

Across four decades, thousands of families have trusted BMH with their most precious moments — restoring sight, welcoming new life, reclaiming mental health, and transforming confidence. That trust, renewed every generation, is our most meaningful credential.

The NHS Difference

What Does NHS Training
Actually Mean?

Evidence-based protocols — every clinical decision grounded in peer-reviewed evidence and outcome data, not habit or tradition.

Patient safety culture — structured checklists, complication protocols, and outcome auditing are standard practice, not optional extras.

Volume and complexity — NHS consultants handle some of the highest surgical volumes in the world. That translates to genuine expertise, not just credentials.

Brought back, not just visited — our specialists didn't just attend a conference abroad. They worked as NHS consultants. When they returned to Chandigarh, they brought those living protocols with them.

THE UK / NHS ADVANTAGE

Why It Matters That Our Doctors Trained in the NHS

The NHS is the world's most scrutinised public health system — accountable to Parliament, audited annually, and subject to independent clinical review. Training there is not a marketing statement. It is a verifiable standard.

Our ophthalmology and neurology specialists completed their higher specialist training in NHS hospitals — working alongside senior NHS consultants, managing complex cases, and operating under the same governance frameworks that make the NHS a global benchmark for quality.

That background is woven into every clinic at BMH: how we document, how we follow up, how we manage complications, and how we explain options to patients and families. International training is not a credential we display — it is how we work.

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Our Values

Four principles that have guided every decision at BMH for forty years.

Excellence

We benchmark our outcomes against international standards, not local averages. Being the best in Chandigarh is not our ambition — being as good as the best in the world is.

Compassion

Every patient is a family, not a case number. We take the time to explain, to listen, and to treat the person behind the diagnosis with the dignity every human being deserves.

Innovation

We were first with LASIK. First with biofeedback. First with MIGS glaucoma surgery in Chandigarh. We will continue to be first — because our patients deserve the best available medicine, not last decade's standard of care.

Trust

Forty years of families returning to us for the most important moments of their lives — sight, birth, mental health, recovery — is our greatest credential. We do not take that trust lightly.
