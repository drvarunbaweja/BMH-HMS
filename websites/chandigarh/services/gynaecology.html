FEATURED: PAINLESS DELIVERY

Labour Without Fear — Epidural Analgesia at BMH

Epidural analgesia is the gold standard for pain relief during labour, widely practiced across the UK, USA, and Europe — and now available at BMH Chandigarh under the expert guidance of Dr. Namrata Baweja.

  • How it works: A small catheter is placed in the epidural space of the lower back, delivering controlled pain medication that keeps you comfortable while remaining conscious, alert, and able to push.

  • Proven safety record: Epidurals have been used in millions of deliveries worldwide. The dose is carefully titrated — enough to relieve pain, not enough to affect baby or your ability to push.

  • International standard: Over 70% of deliveries in the UK's NHS are performed with epidural analgesia. Dr. Namrata brings this same standard of care to Chandigarh.

  • Dr. Namrata's expertise: With 15+ years in OB/GYN and 2000+ deliveries, Dr. Namrata has championed painless delivery as the compassionate, evidence-based choice for modern obstetric care.

MYTHS vs FACTS

Setting the Record Straight on Painless Delivery

Misinformation has kept many women from accessing safe, effective pain relief during labour. Here is the evidence.

MYTH

Epidurals cause permanent back pain after delivery.

FACT

Large-scale studies show no link between epidurals and long-term back pain. Post-delivery back soreness is common regardless of pain relief method and resolves on its own.

MYTH

The epidural affects the baby and is harmful.

FACT

The concentration used in obstetric epidurals is extremely low and is absorbed minimally by the baby. Millions of healthy babies are born every year following epidural analgesia.

MYTH

If you have an epidural, you cannot push during delivery.

FACT

Modern "walking epidurals" preserve motor function. You feel pressure and can push effectively. Epidurals reduce pain — not your ability to participate in labour.

MYTH

Painless delivery always ends in a C-section.

FACT

Epidural analgesia does not increase the rate of Caesarean sections. Many women who choose painless delivery have completely normal vaginal births.

MYTH

Epidurals slow down labour significantly.

FACT

While an epidural may slightly extend the second stage of labour, this is managed expertly by the obstetric team. The trade-off in patient comfort and reduced stress hormones is well worth it.

MYTH

Natural delivery is always better than painless delivery.

FACT

Both choices are valid. Pain relief during labour does not make you less of a mother. A positive birth experience — whatever form that takes — is what matters most.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder affecting 1 in 8 women of reproductive age. Symptoms include irregular periods, excess androgen levels, and polycystic ovaries on ultrasound. Treatment is tailored to your goals — lifestyle management, hormonal regulation, and fertility treatment if conception is desired. BMH offers comprehensive PCOS management.

The first step is a detailed consultation with Dr. Namrata, where she takes a thorough medical and reproductive history from both partners. This is followed by baseline blood tests (hormonal profile), an ultrasound to assess ovarian reserve, and semen analysis. Together, these investigations help map out the most appropriate and least invasive treatment plan.

Laparoscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure performed under general anaesthesia. Unlike open surgery, it uses tiny incisions (5–10 mm) and a camera, resulting in less pain, faster recovery (usually 1–2 days), minimal scarring, and lower infection risk. It is used for diagnosis and treatment of conditions such as endometriosis, ovarian cysts, fibroids, and unexplained infertility.
